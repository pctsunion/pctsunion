Belittled Teachers

Educated reader, you who are always in search of knowledge, enjoy this special edition of the newsletter of the teachers' union. This edition addresses some points made at the staff meeting last Wednesday and to help you learn about your rights as an independent contractor who receives a 1099 tax form.

Let's start with the meeting points.

  1. First Point ….. we really feel the tears of management and apologize, as our goal is to provide teachers with a voice that can't be persecuted or punished, but only heard. We also cry every time we get sick and are faced with the dilemma of having to go to work sick because otherwise we don't get paid. We cry when we have a personal situation since we know that in the process of dealing with the situation, if we are absent, we will not collect either. We cry when after more than two years of loyalty to the school our salaries stay the same, while everything else goes up. We also cry when we are subjected to having large numbers of students in classrooms without the necessary resources.
  2. Second Point ….. management instead of providing job security conveyed the opposite by threatening to close the school and saying that we were all replaceable. Management forgot in their speech that they are also replaceable, this school WILL NOT BE CLOSED, we teachers have the preparation and experience to make it work. So this workshop will not be closed, at most it will change its name. So if management doesn't want to be a part of it, this won't be a story of Business Closure, it will be a story of Restoration and Renovation.

Now let's talk about your rights as a teacher who receives a 1099 tax form. If you receive a 1099 tax form it means that in addition to being a teacher you are an independent contractor. This means that in the law of the United States and Florida there are two core rights for you.

  1. First Right ….. Right to Independent Working Conditions - Independent contractors have the right to determine when and where they work, meaning that a company can't make an independent contractor work in a particular location or at set hours. The contractor has the right to set rates, although a company can opt not to hire a contractor based on those rates. The contractor agrees to deliver a specific product or service by a specific date.
  2. Second Right ….. Right to a Contract - An independent contractor can write a contract that specifies payment, including how much and the timeline for payment, as well as details of the job they are being contracted for and termination conditions. The contract should define the contractor's work as that of an independent contractor's, not an employee. If this contract is broken, both parts have the right to sue for breach of contract.

Thank you for all the information sent and for the help in the translation. Keep sending!!!!! Soon we will have more anonymous channels to communicate and share safely.

We are a team!

谢谢

Toxic Boss Disorder Quiz (TBD)

Perhaps while working at school during the day you sing in your mind the song "Should I Stay or Should I Go" by "The Clash". Maybe it's because you're suffering from Toxic Boss Disorder.

Swedish researchers at the Stress Institute at Stockholm University studied more than 3,100 humans over a 10-year period in typical work settings and found that workers’ risks for angina, heart attack, and death rose along with having worked for toxic bosses.

Use The Toxic Boss Disorder Quiz To Help You Decide to Lead or Leave this school.

This is the Official Assessment Professionals Use to Identify TBD (Toxic Boss Disorder ) Symptoms at Work Adapted for General Use.

You, educated reader, can search for any variation of Toxic Boss Disorder Quiz online and do it. We are sure that the results will be the same.

Here’s How to Take The Toxic Boss Quiz

  1. The Toxic Boss Quiz has only seven questions.
  2. Pick only one of the two answers for each question as your choice (A or B).
  3. Choose the answer that most feels like your situation.
  4. Each answer will have a point assigned to it.
  5. Add up the points.
  6. If the points add up to 7 or less, your boss is not toxic.
  7. If the points add up to 8 or more, your boss is toxic.

Are You Ready? Let’s Go.

(I) ONE
A. Does your boss care more about YOUR success than their own? If so, add 1 point.
B. Does your boss PRETEND TO CARE, but care more about themselves? If so, add 2 points.

(II) TWO
A. Does your boss 1) talk KINDLY about people behind their back, 2) never play kiss up and 3) confront hard issues while 4) taking 100% responsibility for their life? If so, add 1 point.
B. Does your boss talk about people behind their back, but when confronted they play kiss up to get gain? If so, add 2 points.

(III) THREE
A. Does your boss TALK STRAIGHT with others when having crucial conversations or making critical decisions? If so, add 1 point.
B. Does your boss blame committees when making decisions to protect their own hidden agenda? If so, add 2 points.

(IV) FOUR
A. Does your boss demonstrate high-character ethic, confidence and results bundled with humility, while shedding pride, ego and self-importance? If so, add 1 point.
B. Does your boss put on the face of humility when pride and ego drive their personality ethic? If so, add 2 points.

(V) FIVE
A. Does your boss utilize merit, facts with perspective, truth and kindness as a reasoning method? If so, add 1 point.
B. Does your boss use fear, self-importance or claim “fairness” as a reasoning method (when the “appearance of fairness,” not actual fairness, is the true intent) to maintain power at great lengths? If so, add 2 points.

(VI) SIX
A. Does your boss utilize relationships to foster transformational experiences at work to give more to you and others? If so, add 1 point.
B. Does your boss twist relationships to use them as transactions to “get” instead of transformations to give? If so, add 2 points.

(VII) SEVEN
A. Does your boss seek ways to BUILD YOU UP and give you credit especially in front of “important” people? If so, add 1 point.
B. Does your boss seek ways to take credit and put you down, especially in front of “important” people, as a way to get personal gain for fear of losing credibility (as a result of your success and potential workplace power-shift dynamic)? If so, add 2 points.

You be the one to judge your relationship with this management and not us!

Leadership Warning Signs

Every failing school exhibits warning signs that require action on the part of the school principal or management. Individually these signs may not indicate impending failure; collectively, however, they clearly show that the school is either on the verge of failure or headed in that direction.

According to studies, the 8 warning signs are primarily associated with administrative or institutional leadership. These are the signs:

  1. A high turnover of teachers (at least 3 teachers or assistant teachers) every 6 months.
  2. No one appears to be in charge, i.e.; when the school principal or management is present, the office door is usually closed.
  3. The school principal or management does not include members of her/his staff (If the staff exists) in the strategic planning process.
  4. The school principal or management has an authoritarian style of leadership and teachers are afraid to approach and/or talk to her/him ( i.e.; teachers do not decide by vote if someone is going to be fired or which curriculum is the best).
  5. The school principal or management is afraid of confrontation and avoids making decisions on issues that are, or appear to be, controversial.
  6. Ineffective employees (teachers, assistant, helpers) are tolerated, and no action is taken to remove them.
  7. The school principal or management does not visit classrooms and does not provide constructive feedback to teachers.
  8. The school principal or management does not acknowledge or recognize students and teachers who are doing a good job.

A school’s failure should never be seen as surprising or inevitable.

"Let's table this." The warning signs are neither difficult to discern nor impossible to correct. There are no excuses for school failure, and waiting until the end of the academic year to realize that the school has failed simply means that the management have consciously allowed our children to be left behind.

Intelligent reader, considering all this, you be the judge of which direction this school is headed.

In this school, more than needing a teachers' union, a management with an education in pedagogy is urgently needed.
